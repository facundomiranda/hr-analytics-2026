# Business Recommendations

## Recommendation 1: Address Company-Wide Employee Attrition

### Insight

The company experiences an attrition rate of 16.21%, meaning approximately 1 in every 6 employees leaves the organization.

### Recommendation

HR leadership should prioritize employee retention initiatives by identifying the primary drivers of turnover through employee feedback, exit interviews, and engagement surveys.

### Goal

Reduce overall attrition from 16.21% to below 12% within the next 12 months.

---

## Recommendation 2: Focus Retention Efforts on Research & Development and Sales

### Insight

Research & Development accounts for 133 employee departures and Sales accounts for 92 departures, representing the majority of company attrition.

### Recommendation

Conduct department-level deep dives to identify turnover drivers such as workload, management effectiveness, career progression opportunities, and employee satisfaction.

### Goal

Reduce attrition within Research & Development and Sales departments by 15% over the next year.

---

## Recommendation 3: Evaluate Compensation Competitiveness

### Insight

Employees who remained at the company earn an average monthly income of $6,833, while employees who left earned only $4,787 on average.

### Recommendation

Review compensation structures, promotion pathways, and salary competitiveness to ensure employees perceive long-term financial growth opportunities within the organization.

### Goal

Improve retention among lower-income employee segments and reduce salary-related turnover over the next 12 months.

---

## Recommendation 4: Improve Retention of Early-Career Employees

### Insight

Employees under 40 account for 180 of the 237 total employee departures, representing more than 75% of all attrition.

### Recommendation

Strengthen onboarding programs, mentorship opportunities, career development plans, and internal mobility pathways for early-career employees.

### Goal

Reduce attrition among employees under 40 by at least 15% within the next year.

---

## Recommendation 5: Investigate Attrition Among Key Educational Backgrounds

### Insight

Employees with Life Sciences and Medical educational backgrounds account for 152 employee departures, representing the largest concentration of attrition across all education groups.

### Recommendation

Conduct further analysis to determine whether turnover is driven by compensation, role fit, career advancement limitations, or external market demand for these skill sets.

### Goal

Identify root causes of turnover within these employee segments and implement targeted retention strategies within the next two quarters.

---

## Recommendation 6: Increase Employee Retention During the First Five Years

### Insight

Employees who leave the company stay an average of only 5 years before exiting.

### Recommendation

Develop retention programs focused on employees during their first five years, including career development planning, manager coaching, performance feedback, and advancement opportunities.

### Goal

Increase average employee tenure from 5 years to at least 6 years over the next 18 months.

### Summary

Employees with lower compensation and those in earlier career stages are significantly more likely to leave the organization. 
Retention efforts should prioritize career growth opportunities, compensation reviews, and employee development programs targeting these high-risk groups.
