# HR Analytics SQL Challenges

## Project Objective

The goal of this project is to analyze employee workforce data and generate actionable HR insights using SQL.

The analysis focuses on employee attrition, compensation, demographics, education, and tenure to better understand workforce trends and potential retention challenges.

---

## Business Questions

### 1. Employee Attrition Rate

Calculate the percentage of employees who have left the company compared to the total workforce.

**Business Value:**
Provides a high-level view of employee turnover and overall workforce stability.

---

### 2. Department Attrition Analysis

Determine the number of employees who left from each department.

**Business Value:**
Identifies departments experiencing the highest turnover and potential retention issues.

---

### 3. Compensation and Attrition

Compare the average monthly income of employees who left the company versus those who remained.

**Business Value:**
Helps evaluate whether compensation may be related to employee turnover.

---

### 4. Age Group Attrition Analysis

Segment employees into age groups and analyze attrition counts within each category.

Age Groups:

* Under 30
* 30–39
* 40–49
* 50+

**Business Value:**
Highlights workforce demographics that may be at higher risk of leaving.

---

### 5. Education Field Attrition

Identify which education fields have the highest number of employees leaving the company.

**Business Value:**
Helps HR understand whether attrition is concentrated among employees with specific educational backgrounds.

---

### 6. Employee Tenure Before Exit

Calculate the average number of years employees stayed with the company before leaving.

**Business Value:**
Measures workforce retention and helps identify whether turnover occurs early or later in the employee lifecycle.
